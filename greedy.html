Greedy Algorithms

Make locally optimal choices to find global solutions

What Makes an Algorithm Greedy?

Greedy algorithms are a powerful problem-solving approach that makes the best choice at each step, hoping that these local choices will lead to a globally optimal solution. While this might seem counterintuitive, there are many problems where making the locally optimal choice at each step actually leads to the best overall solution.

Key Concepts

  • Local Optimality: Greedy algorithms build up a solution by choosing the best local option at each step.
  • Global Optimality: For a greedy algorithm to be valid, local optimality must lead to global optimality.
